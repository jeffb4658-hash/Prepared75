Must-Have Bug Out Bag Essentials for Survival

A bug out bag is a essential component of any emergency preparedness plan. It's a portable collection of supplies that can help you survive for at least 72 hours in the event of a natural disaster, power outage, or other crisis. In this article, we'll explore the must-have bug out bag essentials that you should include in your kit.

Water and Food

Having access to clean drinking water and non-perishable food is crucial for survival. Consider including the following items in your bug out bag:

  • Water filter or purification tablets: A water filter or purification tablets can help ensure that you have access to clean drinking water, even in emergency situations.
  • Non-perishable food: Include energy-rich foods like nuts, dried fruits, and jerky, as well as ready-to-eat meals like MREs.

We recommend the LifeStraw Personal Water Filter for its convenience and effectiveness.

Shelter and Warmth

Adequate shelter and warmth are essential for survival. Consider including the following items in your bug out bag:

  • Tent or tarp: A lightweight tent or tarp can provide protection from the elements.
  • Sleeping bag or blanket: A warm sleeping bag or blanket can help keep you warm in cold temperatures.

We recommend the MSR Elixir 2-Person Tent for its durability and ease of setup.

First Aid and Hygiene

A basic first aid kit and personal hygiene items are essential for maintaining health and well-being. Consider including the following items in your bug out bag:

  • First aid kit: A basic first aid kit should include items like bandages, antiseptic wipes, and pain relievers.
  • Toilet paper and hand sanitizer: Personal hygiene items like toilet paper and hand sanitizer can help prevent the spread of illness.

We recommend the Adventure Medical Kits Mini First Aid Kit for its compact size and comprehensive contents.

Communication and Navigation

Staying connected and navigating your surroundings are crucial for survival. Consider including the following items in your bug out bag:

  • Cell phone with charger and back-up battery: A cell phone can be a vital tool for communication, but be sure to include a charger and back-up battery.
  • Compass and map: A compass and map can help you navigate your surroundings and find your way to safety.

We recommend the Anker PowerCore 20000 Portable Power Bank for its high capacity and compact size.

Buying Guide

When selecting items for your bug out bag, consider the following factors:

  • Quality and durability: Choose items that are well-made and can withstand rough handling.
  • Weight and size: Select items that are lightweight and compact to minimize bulk.
  • Cost and value: Consider the cost of each item and whether it provides good value for the price.

By following these guidelines and including the must-have bug out bag essentials outlined in this article, you can create a comprehensive and effective emergency kit.
